# Create-HR-Dashboard-with-Tableau...

Project Overview

This project focuses on creating an interactive HR analytics dashboard using Tableau to help organizations make data-driven decisions about their workforce. The dashboard visualizes key HR metrics such as employee demographics, attrition rate, performance, salary distribution, and departmental trends, providing a comprehensive overview of organizational health.

Objectives

To analyze and visualize key HR performance indicators.
To identify workforce trends and employee retention patterns.
To present insights that can support data-driven HR strategies.
To enhance executive decision-making through clear visual storytelling.

Dashboard Features:-

The HR Dashboard includes the following analytical sections:
Employee Overview – Displays total employees, gender ratio, and headcount by department.
Attrition Analysis – Shows attrition rate, reasons for leaving, and patterns across job roles or experience levels.
Salary Insights – Visualizes salary distribution by job role, department, and education level.
Performance Metrics – Highlights performance ratings and identifies top-performing departments.
Demographic Analysis – Breaks down employees by age, education, and marital status.
Filters & Interactivity – Enables filtering by department, job role, education, or gender for customized analysis.

Tools and Technologies:-

Tableau Desktop / Tableau Public – For data visualization and dashboard creation.
Microsoft Excel / CSV Dataset – For raw HR data preparation.
PowerPoint / Canva (Optional) – For presenting dashboard findings.

Dataset Description:-

The dataset used in this project contains employee-level information including:
Employee ID
Age
Gender
Department
Job Role
Education
Monthly Income
Performance Rating
Attrition Status
Years at Company
Note: The dataset is fictional and used for educational and analytical purposes only.

Steps Involved:-

Data Collection: Imported HR dataset from Excel/CSV into Tableau.
Data Cleaning: Removed duplicates, handled missing values, and standardized columns.
Data Modeling: Established calculated fields (e.g., attrition rate, average salary).
Visualization Design: Created KPIs, charts, and interactive filters.
Dashboard Assembly: Combined all visuals into a cohesive and interactive dashboard layout.
Insight Generation: Derived meaningful HR insights and recommendations.

Key Insights:-

The highest attrition was observed in the Sales Department with employees having 1–3 years of experience.
R&D and Finance Departments showed the highest employee satisfaction levels.
Average salary tended to increase with education level and tenure.
Gender distribution was skewed toward males in technical roles.

Conclusion

This HR Dashboard empowers HR managers and decision-makers to identify workforce trends, improve employee retention, and align HR strategies with organizational goals. By visualizing data effectively, it turns complex HR information into actionable insights.

The HR Dashboard serves as a powerful analytical tool designed to transform raw employee data into meaningful business insights. Through interactive visualizations and key performance indicators, it enables HR professionals, business leaders, and analysts to monitor workforce dynamics in real time.

By integrating metrics such as attrition rate, employee performance, salary trends, and demographic distribution, the dashboard offers a holistic view of organizational health. Decision-makers can quickly identify problem areas, recognize high-performing teams, and implement targeted retention or hiring strategies.

In today’s data-driven corporate environment, such visual analytics play a crucial role in strategic HR planning. They allow organizations to:
Predict attrition risks and proactively address employee turnover.
Align workforce composition with company goals and diversity targets.
Evaluate compensation fairness and performance-based incentives.
Strengthen employee engagement and satisfaction through informed interventions.
Ultimately, this project demonstrates how data visualization and analytics can empower HR departments to move beyond manual reporting and towards strategic, evidence-based decision-making. The Tableau dashboard not only highlights current workforce trends but also provides a foundation for predictive HR analytics in future iterations.
